TAJ MAHAL

The Taj Mahal, located in Agra, India, is an exquisite white marble mausoleum built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife, Mumtaz Mahal. This magnificent structure, completed in 1648, is one of the greatest examples of Mughal architecture and a symbol of eternal love. The Taj Mahal stands on the banks of the Yamuna River and is surrounded by a lush charbagh (Mughal garden). The intricate inlay work, marble lattice screens, and Persian calligraphy inscriptions make it a masterpiece. üìú History & Legacy

The construction of the Taj Mahal began in 1632 and took over 20 years to complete, with the labor of more than 20,000 artisans. The finest materials were sourced from across Asia, including white marble from Rajasthan, jade from China, and turquoise from Tibet. Today, the Taj Mahal is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a global symbol of love and devotion.

üåü Architectural Brilliance

The Taj Mahal is an architectural wonder with its perfect symmetry, intricate floral designs, and reflective pools. The marble dome appears weightless as it gracefully floats above the main structure. Its four minarets, built at a slight outward tilt, ensure they do not collapse on the main tomb in case of an earthquake.
