ARUNACHALAM

Arunachalam, also known as Tiruvannamalai, is a sacred town in Tamil Nadu, India, renowned for its spiritual significance and natural beauty. It is home to the Arunachaleswarar Temple, one of the largest Shiva temples in the world, dedicated to Lord Shiva in the form of a lingam. The town is dominated by Mount Arunachala, a holy hill considered to be a manifestation of Lord Shiva himself. Devotees perform Girivalam, a 14-kilometer circumambulation of the hill, as an act of devotion. Arunachalam is also closely associated with the sage Ramana Maharshi, who attained enlightenment here. The town hosts the grand Karthigai Deepam festival, where millions of lamps are lit, creating a mesmerizing spectacle. With its serene atmosphere, spiritual energy, and rich cultural heritage, Arunachalam is a must-visit destination for seekers of peace and divinity. üôèüõï
