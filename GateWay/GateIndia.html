GATEWAY OF INDIA

The Gateway of India, located in Mumbai, Maharashtra, is a majestic arch-monument that stands as a symbol of India's rich colonial history and architectural excellence. Built in 1924 to commemorate the visit of **King George V and Queen Mary**, it overlooks the stunning **Arabian Sea**. This grand structure, designed in the **Indo-Saracenic style**, was later used as the departure point for British troops, marking the end of British rule in India. Today, it is one of the most visited landmarks in Mumbai, attracting tourists, historians, and photographers from around the world. üáÆüá≥‚ú®

üìú Historical Significance

Built during the **British Raj**, the Gateway of India was completed in 1924 and became a key landmark of Mumbai. The last British troops departed India through this gateway on **February 28, 1948**, making it a historic site of India's independence.

üèõ Architectural Brilliance

The Gateway of India features a **26-meter-high basalt arch**, blending **Hindu, Muslim, and European architectural styles**. Its grand design and strategic location by the sea make it a stunning sight, especially at sunset.
