MUNNAR

Located in the Western Ghats of Kerala, Munnar is a picturesque hill station known for its tea plantations, lush greenery, and misty hills. The region is often referred to as the Kashmir of South India due to its breathtaking landscapes and cool climate. Munnar is home to attractions like Eravikulam National Park, Mattupetty Dam, and Tea Museum, which showcase the region's natural beauty and rich history. The best time to visit Munnar is from September to May, when the weather is cool and pleasant. Whether you're interested in trekking, exploring tea estates, or simply soaking in the mesmerizing views, Munnar has something for every traveler!

Munnar is a haven for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts. The Anamudi Peak, the highest peak in South India, offers challenging trekking trails, while the Kundala Lake is perfect for boating and picnics.

üåø Tea Plantations and Spice Gardens

Munnar is famous for its tea plantations, which produce some of the finest tea in India. Visitors can explore the tea estates or take a stroll through the spice gardens, which house a variety of exotic spices.

üö∂ Trekking and Adventure

Munnar offers numerous trekking trails, including the Anamudi Trek and Chokramudi Trek, which provide stunning views of the Western Ghats.

üå∫ Wildlife and Nature

Munnar is home to diverse wildlife, including the endangered Nilgiri Tahr. The Eravikulam National Park and Chinnar Wildlife Sanctuary are must-visit destinations for wildlife enthusiasts.
