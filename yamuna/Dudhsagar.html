Dudhsagar Falls – The Sea of Milk

DUDHSAGAR FALLS

Dudhsagar Falls, located on the Goa-Karnataka border, is one of India's tallest waterfalls, standing at 310 meters (1017 feet). The name "Dudhsagar" translates to "Sea of Milk," as the water cascading down the rocky cliffs resembles a milky white flow.

The falls are surrounded by lush green forests and are part of the Bhagwan Mahaveer Sanctuary and Mollem National Park. The best time to visit is during the monsoon season (June to September) when the falls are in full flow.

To reach Dudhsagar, you can take a train to Castle Rock or Kulem station and then trek through the forest. Alternatively, jeep safaris are available from Mollem. The trek is adventurous and offers stunning views of the Western Ghats.

Dudhsagar is also famous for its railway bridge, which offers a breathtaking view of the falls. Many trains passing through this route slow down, allowing passengers to enjoy the majestic sight.

Trekking to Dudhsagar

The trek to Dudhsagar Falls is an exhilarating experience. The trail takes you through dense forests, streams, and rocky terrain. It's a moderate-level trek, and the journey is as rewarding as the destination.

Monsoon Magic

During the monsoon, Dudhsagar is at its most spectacular. The increased water flow creates a thunderous roar, and the mist from the falls adds to the magical atmosphere. However, trekking during this time requires caution due to slippery paths.

Wildlife and Nature

The area around Dudhsagar is rich in biodiversity. You might spot wildlife like deer, monkeys, and various bird species. The lush greenery and pristine environment make it a paradise for nature lovers.
