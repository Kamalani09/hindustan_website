KONARK SUN TEMPLE

The Konark Sun Temple, located in Konark, Odisha, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of India's most magnificent temples. Built in the 13th century by King **Narasimha Deva I**, this temple is dedicated to the **Sun God, Surya**. Designed in the form of a giant **chariot with twelve intricately carved wheels** and **seven stone horses**, it represents the celestial chariot of the Sun. Despite partial ruins, its architectural brilliance continues to mesmerize visitors worldwide. üèõ‚ú®

üìú Historical Significance

The **Konark Sun Temple** was built in **1250 AD** by King **Narasimha Deva I** of the Eastern Ganga dynasty. The temple, once an important pilgrimage site, is believed to have been positioned strategically to allow the first rays of the sun to illuminate the deity inside.

üèõ Architectural Brilliance

The temple is shaped like a massive **chariot with 24 ornately carved wheels**, each serving as a sundial. The intricate carvings on its walls depict scenes from daily life, mythology, and celestial beings, showcasing the artistic excellence of ancient India.
